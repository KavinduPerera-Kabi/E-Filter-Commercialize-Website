Research Problem

In the film industry, creating a captivating narrative based on historical or real events necessitates extensive research and resource utilization. When a director and their film crew embark on the journey of planning a new movie or drama, their initial step involves a deep dive into the concept they aim to bring to life. This comprehensive research encompasses the vast expanse of digitized information resources available today. Collaborative discussions between the director and the scriptwriter are vital in defining the vision for the audience.

Summary Generator: Our proposed solution aims to streamline information acquisition. The system harnesses video resources as the primary input, processing them to generate concise, informative summaries. These summaries encapsulate the video's key elements, ensuring that only relevant information is included, all achieved through a filtering mechanism. This approach not only saves time and effort but also provides directors and writers with a robust foundation for narrative development.

Topics Generator: In addition to summary generation, our system features the capability to identify and extract topics discussed within the video content. This functionality allows for efficient navigation and reference to specific subject matters within the video, offering a valuable resource for directors seeking to maintain storyline continuity and ensure historical accuracy.

Question and Answer Generator: Our system further enhances content creation by generating pertinent questions and answers based on the video's content. This function is invaluable for scriptwriters and directors who aim to deepen the narrative and engage the audience with thought-provoking and informative content. It not only streamlines the question-generation process but also ensures that the queries align closely with the video's themes and subject matter.

Methadology

E-FILTERING is a versatile system designed for filmmakers, offering efficient video analysis and segmentation based on topics. By taking video resources as input, it saves time and effort by providing directors and writers with organized segments for easy movie production. The system includes functions for generating subtitles, summarized notes, questions, and answers.

The automatic video segmentation utilizes a linguistic approach with video transcripts, using the AssemblyAI API for transcription with timestamps. The TopicTiling algorithm identifies topic transitions based on linguistic elements, allowing the system to split the video into smaller, topic-specific segments. While researchers have explored linguistic methods for video segmentation, challenges such as transcription accuracy and algorithm robustness persist.

In addition to segmentation, E-FILTERING also generates questions and answers from documentary videos. This involves a multi-step process, including video processing, audio recognition, and natural language processing. The system analyzes visual elements, transforms visual data into a format understandable by language processing algorithms, and converts spoken words into accurate transcriptions using automatic speech recognition (ASR) algorithms. The generated questions are pertinent to the video content, and the system employs clustering techniques for related queries. The final step involves using NLP algorithms to generate accurate and relevant responses in various formats.

Overall, E-FILTERING presents a comprehensive solution for filmmakers, streamlining the video analysis, segmentation, and content generation processes.
